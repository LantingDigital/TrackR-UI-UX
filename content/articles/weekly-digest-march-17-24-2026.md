A massive week. Every corner of the industry had something worth paying attention to, and a couple of these stories are going to echo for months.

## Tormenta Rampaging Run Completes Track

The biggest hardware story of the week: Six Flags Over Texas placed the final piece of track on Tormenta Rampaging Run on March 13, completing the layout of what is being billed as the world's first giga dive coaster. The ride is expected to break six world records when it opens later in 2026, including tallest, fastest, and longest dive coaster on the planet.

If you have not been following this one, Tormenta is a massive vertical-drop coaster with a giga-scale layout that goes way beyond the typical B&M dive formula. The vertical loop topped off earlier in the year, and seeing the full track circuit complete means testing should be coming soon. The Arlington park has been building toward this for a while, and the scale of the thing in construction photos is hard to process. This is going to be a destination ride.

For the record: this is the second time in two weeks that a Six Flags park has had a major coaster milestone. The company is spending.

## Great Adventure Gets Two Coasters, Not One

Six Flags Great Adventure dropped two coaster bombs this week. On March 19, vertical construction began on the park's 2027 launched coaster, with the first pieces of blue track now rising over Jackson, New Jersey. That one has been expected for a while.

The surprise was the second announcement: Theme Park Tribune reported that Great Adventure is also getting an additional coaster, separate from the launched coaster project. That brings the park's future coaster total to 14. The new ride is expected to tie into the re-imagined Boardwalk area that is opening by late spring 2026, potentially serving as the gateway to the 2027 coaster zone.

Two major coaster projects at the same park, running in parallel. Great Adventure has not seen this level of investment in years. Whether the new Six Flags corporate strategy and the EPR park sales are driving this urgency is an open question, but the result for guests is clear: Great Adventure is about to have a very good couple of years.

## Six Flags Corporate: Activist Investors and Park Sales

Speaking of EPR, the fallout from Six Flags' March 5 announcement that it is selling seven parks to EPR Properties continued to dominate the business side of the conversation. On March 13, Screamscape reported that activist investor JANA Partners is ramping up pressure on Six Flags leadership, just days after the company also announced NFL star Travis Kelce as a 2026 brand ambassador.

On r/rollercoasters, the discussion has been split between people excited about the new capital investment (Tormenta, Great Adventure projects) and people worried about what the park sales mean for the seven properties being transferred. When parks change ownership, maintenance budgets, staffing, and ride investment can shift unpredictably. The enthusiast community has seen this movie before, and not all of those stories ended well.

The Travis Kelce partnership is a different kind of move. It is a clear play for GP attention and mainstream cultural relevance. Whether that translates to actual attendance bumps or just social media impressions remains to be seen.

## Disney: Bluey Gets a Date, Muppets Take the Coaster

Two big Disney developments this week.

**Bluey's Wild World** at Disney's Animal Kingdom now has an official opening date: May 26, 2026. The attraction rethemes the former Rafiki's Planet Watch area and Wildlife Express Train route, which closed in late February to make way for the new experience. Bluey is becoming a genuine global theme park franchise. Disney World's new offering joins Bluey's Best Day Ever at Disneyland and the Bluey the Ride family coaster debuting at Alton Towers later this month. For a show that started as an Australian children's cartoon, the speed at which Bluey has become a park headliner is remarkable.

**Rock 'n' Roller Coaster Starring The Muppets** is moving forward at Hollywood Studios. The original Rock 'n' Roller Coaster Starring Aerosmith had its last day of operation on March 1, and Disney confirmed the Muppets overlay will debut by summer 2026. The rethemed ride will be a Tier 1 Lightning Lane Multi Pass attraction with Early Entry and Extended Evening Hours access for resort guests.

The Muppets retheme continues to divide the online community. Losing Aerosmith stings for people who grew up with the ride. But the ride hardware (the Vekoma launch coaster with inversions) is not changing, and the Muppets humor could give the ride a personality that the aging Aerosmith overlay had lost. Hollywood Studios needs every attraction it can keep running right now, with Rise of the Resistance dealing with extended downtime and a relatively thin overall lineup.

Also worth noting: at Disney's annual shareholders meeting, Bob Iger formally retired and Disney Parks chief Josh D'Amaro stepped into a larger leadership role. Disney Cruise Line also announced its next Wish-class ship, Disney Believe. The parks and experiences portfolio is being consolidated under D'Amaro in a way that suggests the theme park division is going to be even more central to Disney's business going forward.

## Universal: Stardust Racers Testing, Fast & Furious Takes Shape

Over at Universal, Epic Universe's Stardust Racers dueling Mack Rides coaster has begun testing in Orlando. This is a big step toward the new park's opening, though Screamscape noted that the ride faced a brief refurbishment delay earlier in March. Complex dueling coaster systems have a long history of operational challenges (see: Dueling Dragons, which ran one side only for years before being demolished), so seeing test runs is encouraging.

At Universal Studios Florida, construction continues on Fast & Furious: Hollywood Drift, an Intamin multi-launch coaster with 360-degree drifting cars that hit up to 72 mph. The ride was previewed at IAAPA 2025 and is on track for a 2026 opening. Between this and Velocicoaster, Universal Orlando's coaster lineup is going to be extremely strong.

## SeaWorld: Barracuda Strike Testing, Kumba Down Again

SeaWorld San Antonio reached a milestone with its new Barracuda Strike multi-launch coaster completing test runs. The Premier Rides layout has been circulating in footage shared by Screamscape, and the ride looks solid. San Antonio has been quietly building a respectable coaster collection.

Less encouraging: Busch Gardens Tampa's Kumba, the legendary B&M looper, was closed again in March as crews work through an extended refurbishment. Kumba is one of the most important B&M coasters in the world from a historical perspective (it was one of the first sitdown loopers B&M built), and extended downtime on a ride that old always raises questions about how much structural work is needed. The park has not said much publicly about the scope of the refurbishment, which is either reassuring (routine maintenance) or concerning (more serious issues they do not want to discuss). We will see.

## International Notes

A few stories from outside the US:

**Toverland** (Netherlands) received financial support from local government for future expansion. The Dutch park has been on a growth trajectory and the public funding gives them runway for new attractions.

**Chessington World of Adventures** (UK) confirmed a full Minecraft World expansion for 2027. Another deep-IP themed land joining the arms race. Between Minecraft, Bluey, and the existing franchises, the question of which IP gets a park land next has become one of the more interesting industry prediction games.

## What's Coming Next Week

March 29 is stacked. Disney Adventure World (the rebranded Walt Disney Studios Park at Disneyland Paris) opens with World of Frozen and Raiponce Tangled Spin. Universal Studios Hollywood reopens Super Silly Fun Land's wet zone. And Disneyland closes its Monorail and Roger Rabbit's Car Toon Spin for extended renovations with no announced reopening dates.

Early reports from Disney Adventure World will be the thing to watch. If the quality matches Tokyo's Fantasy Springs (and early photos suggest it might), Paris could go from the underdog Disney resort to a legitimate destination for park enthusiasts.
