If you've been paying attention to the coaster world over the last two years, you've probably had the same thought as the rest of us: how is every park dropping bangers at the same time?

We're in the middle of something special. The 2024-2026 window has delivered (or is about to deliver) an absurd number of new coasters that aren't just good, they're redefining what parks can do. New ride systems, new manufacturers stepping up, record-breaking builds, and a post-merger Six Flags Entertainment that's throwing money at its parks like it's trying to win something.

Let's run through the highlights.

## 2024: The Warm-Up Year

2024 was a transitional year for a lot of parks. The Six Flags/Cedar Fair merger had just closed, budgets were being reshuffled, and some announced projects got delayed. But there were still some genuinely impressive openings.

**Voltron Nevera at Europa-Park** ran away with the conversation. Mack Rides built their first Stryker coaster, and it immediately won Best New Roller Coaster at the Golden Ticket Awards. Europa-Park has been quietly stacking one of the best coaster lineups in the world for years, and Voltron cemented them as a must-visit for any serious enthusiast.

**Iron Menace at Dorney Park** gave the park its first B&M, a dive coaster that Dorney desperately needed to anchor its lineup. Dive coasters aren't usually the rides that top enthusiast polls, but they're crowd-pleasers and Iron Menace brought some credibility to a park that had been overlooked for new attractions.

**Hyperia at Thorpe Park** was one of the most anticipated openings in Europe. A Mack hyper coaster with a 236-foot drop at 72 degrees, hitting 80 mph. The UK coaster scene had been waiting for a world-class ride at Thorpe for years, and Hyperia delivered. (The opening wasn't without issues, including an early closure for adjustments, but the ride itself earned strong reviews.)

**The Flash at Movie World Australia** brought a launched Mack coaster to the Southern Hemisphere, and **Toutatis at Parc Asterix** continued its strong 2023 reception into its second full season. Not a 2024 opening technically, but it kept showing up in year-end rankings because that Intamin multi-launch is just that good.

## 2025: The Flood

This is the year everything hit at once. Multiple record-breakers, new ride categories, and some parks making the kind of investments that signal a real shift in ambition.

**Stardust Racers at Universal's Epic Universe** is the headliner. When Epic Universe opened on May 22, 2025, Stardust Racers immediately became one of the most talked-about coasters in the world. Manufactured by Mack Rides, the dual-track racing coaster covers over 5,000 feet of track per side, reaches 62 mph, climbs to 133 feet, and features the "Celestial Spin," an inverted crisscross between the two vehicles that has to be seen to be understood. After dark, the onboard lighting package makes the trains look like comets racing across the Orlando sky.

Stardust Racers proved that a themed coaster can be both a legitimate thrill ride and a storytelling experience without compromising either. The enthusiast community has been buzzing about it since opening, and it immediately entered the conversation for best coaster in Florida.

**Siren's Curse at Cedar Point** brought a Vekoma Tilt Coaster to the roller coaster capital of the world. North America's tallest, fastest, and longest tilt coaster, with a hold-and-release moment at the top of the tower that's unlike anything else in Cedar Point's lineup. The tilt mechanism holds the train at an angle before releasing it into the drop, and if you've never experienced that pause at the top, it adds a psychological element that traditional chain lifts can't match.

**Rapterra at Kings Dominion** opened as the world's tallest and longest launched wing coaster. Electromagnetic propulsion launches riders to 65 mph in four seconds, and the wing seat experience (feet dangling, nothing above or below you) makes the inversions feel completely different from a traditional sit-down coaster. Kings Dominion needed a headliner, and Rapterra delivered.

**Georgia Gold Rusher at Six Flags Over Georgia** introduced the world's first Intamin Ultra Surf, a new ride system that had the coaster community genuinely excited. Originally announced for 2024 but delayed due to the challenges of building a prototype, it opened in March 2025 to strong reviews. When a manufacturer debuts an entirely new ride category, that's worth paying attention to.

**Falcon's Flight at Six Flags Qiddiya City** in Saudi Arabia shattered records. The world's tallest roller coaster at 639 feet. The world's fastest at 155 mph. Those numbers are hard to even process. The park opened December 31, 2025, and while most of us haven't made it to Saudi Arabia yet, the POVs and reviews have confirmed that this thing is every bit as insane as the stats suggest.

## 2026: Still Coming

The pipeline for 2026 is stacked, and we're barely into the year.

**Fast & Furious: Hollywood Drift at Universal Studios Hollywood** will be the fastest coaster at any Universal park when it opens, reaching 72 mph on an Intamin ride system with vehicles that can rotate 360 degrees. The ride vehicles are styled after Toretto's 1970 Dodge Charger, which is either perfect or ridiculous depending on how you feel about the franchise. Either way, the engineering is serious.

**Project Rubi at Six Flags Over Texas** is a record-breaking dive roller coaster that's been generating buzz since the announcement. Details are still emerging, but anything that involves Six Flags Over Texas and the word "record-breaking" has the Texas coaster community hyped.

**Universal Kids Resort** is opening near Dallas with two coasters: Jurassic World Cretaceous Coaster and Trollfest Express. The park itself is a smaller-scale concept targeting families, but Universal bringing a new park to Texas is significant for the industry.

And across the Atlantic, **Alton Towers is opening the world's first Bluey roller coaster**, which sounds like it's for kids (it is) but also signals that major IP integrations into coaster attractions are becoming the norm at every level.

## Why This Stretch Matters

Three things are converging to create this golden age:

**Manufacturers are innovating.** Mack's Stryker system. Intamin's Ultra Surf. Vekoma's Tilt Coaster. The major manufacturers aren't just building taller and faster versions of existing models. They're creating entirely new ride categories. That kind of innovation trickles down to every park, because once a new system proves itself, other parks want their own version.

**The merger created spending pressure.** Six Flags Entertainment now operates 40+ parks and needs to give pass holders reasons to visit across the portfolio. That means more new rides, more capital investment, and more competition between parks within the same company. When your pass works at Cedar Point AND Magic Mountain, both parks need to stay relevant.

**Theme parks are mainstream entertainment.** The success of Epic Universe, the global expansion of theme park brands into new markets (Saudi Arabia, Dallas, international destinations), and the growing culture of park content on YouTube and social media have made coasters more visible than ever. Parks are investing accordingly.

## The Takeaway

If you're a thoosie, this is the time to be alive. The number of must-ride new credits over the last three years is genuinely unprecedented. And unlike previous building booms that were concentrated in a few parks, this one is spread across the entire industry. Regional parks, destination parks, international parks. Everyone is building.

Start planning your trips. The 2024-2026 window is going to be one of those stretches that enthusiasts talk about for decades. The coasters opening right now aren't just good additions to their respective parks. They're the kind of rides that reshape your entire top 10.

And we're only halfway through 2026. There's more coming.
