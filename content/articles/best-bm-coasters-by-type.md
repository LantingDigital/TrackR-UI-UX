Bolliger & Mabillard has been building roller coasters since 1990. In that time, they have installed over 150 coasters worldwide, never had a major structural failure, and built some of the most beloved rides in the hobby. Their consistency is legendary. A B&M might not be the most intense or innovative ride at any given park, but it will be smooth, reliable, and forceful for decades.

But B&M builds across a wide range of types, and within each type, the community has strong opinions about which installations are the best. Here is where things stand, based on aggregated rankings from enthusiast polls, Reddit consensus threads, forum discussions, and community voting data.

## Hyper Coasters

B&M's hyper coasters (200+ feet, no inversions, focused on airtime) are arguably their most popular product line. The formula is proven: a tall first drop, a series of airtime hills, a helix or turnaround, and sustained floater airtime throughout.

**Community top picks:**

**Fury 325 (Carowinds)** frequently tops this list, though technically it is classified as a giga (over 300 feet). If you count it as a hyper-class ride, it is the clear favorite. The speed, the sustained forces through the treble clef turnaround, and the sense of flying over the park are unmatched.

**Diamondback (Kings Island)** and **Intimidator (Carowinds)** are both strong traditional hypers. Diamondback gets the edge for its splashdown finale and its consistently strong airtime in every row.

**Nitro (Six Flags Great Adventure)** is the dark horse. It does not have the biggest drop or the most airtime hills, but the ride has a flow to it that makes every element connect. The hammerhead turn has a view of the New Jersey Pine Barrens. The sustained floater in the back row is elite.

**Apollo's Chariot (Busch Gardens Williamsburg)** benefits from the terrain. The drops interact with the hillside, making the height feel bigger than the stats suggest. It was one of the first B&M hypers (opened 1999) and still holds up against newer installations.

**The consensus:** Fury 325 is the best if you count it here. Among pure 200-foot-class hypers, Diamondback and Nitro fight for the top spot depending on who you ask.

## Inverted Coasters

The ride type that put B&M on the map. Riders hang below the track with their feet dangling, and the inversions feel completely different than on a sit-down coaster. B&M inverts are smooth, forceful, and have one of the best ride experiences in the industry.

**Community top picks:**

**Montu (Busch Gardens Tampa)** is the consensus number one. Seven inversions, aggressive pacing, terrain interaction (the ride dips into trenches and through tunnels), and a Batwing element that delivers sustained positive G-forces. Montu has held the top invert spot in most enthusiast polls for over two decades.

**Raptor (Cedar Point)** was the tallest invert when it opened in 1994 and still rides like a top-tier attraction. The cobra roll and the final helix are highlights. The footchoppers through the midway are iconic.

**Afterburn (Carowinds)** is a near-clone of Montu's layout but with a different terrain profile. Enthusiasts who have ridden both often rate Afterburn as the smoother, more consistent ride. It benefits from being at a smaller park with shorter waits.

**Banshee (Kings Island)** is the longest B&M invert ever built (4,124 feet) and one of the newer installations. It is smooth and forceful, but some enthusiasts feel it trades intensity for length. Others love the sustained ride time.

**Alpengiest (Busch Gardens Williamsburg)** was the tallest and fastest invert when it opened in 1997. The ski-resort theming is unique, and the ride through the support structure delivers excellent visuals. The cobra roll is one of the most iconic B&M elements anywhere.

**The consensus:** Montu is king. Raptor and Afterburn fight for second. Alpengeist and Banshee round out the top five.

## Giga Coasters

Only four B&M giga coasters exist worldwide (300+ feet): Millennium Force at Cedar Point, Intimidator 305 at Kings Dominion (technically Intamin, not B&M), Fury 325 at Carowinds, Leviathan at Canada's Wonderland, and Orion at Kings Island. Wait. Let me correct that. Millennium Force and Intimidator 305 are both Intamin. The B&M gigas are Leviathan, Fury 325, and Orion.

**Community top picks:**

**Fury 325 (Carowinds)** is the undisputed best B&M giga and regularly ranks in the top 5 of all steel coasters globally. The treble clef turnaround is signature. The sustained speed through the entire layout is what separates it from other coasters in this height class.

**Leviathan (Canada's Wonderland)** gets criticism for being "short" relative to its height. The ride has a spectacular first drop and strong speed, but the layout after the drop is less eventful than Fury's. Still a top-tier ride by any reasonable standard.

**Orion (Kings Island)** is the controversial one. At 287 feet, it technically does not hit the 300-foot giga threshold by some definitions (the first drop is 300 feet due to terrain, but the lift hill is 287). The ride itself is good but short, and the community was expecting more after years of hype. It is a victim of expectations more than quality.

**The consensus:** Fury 325, by a lot. Leviathan second. Orion third but with an asterisk.

## Dive Coasters

B&M's dive coasters feature a holding brake at the top of the first drop that suspends riders face-down before releasing them into a near-vertical plunge. The first drop is the signature moment. The rest of the ride varies significantly between installations.

**Community top picks:**

**Valravn (Cedar Point)** is the tallest and fastest B&M dive (223 feet, 75 mph). The first drop hold delivers every time. The rest of the layout includes an Immelmann, a second holding drop, and a zero-G roll. Critics say the ride has too many straight sections between elements.

**SheiKra (Busch Gardens Tampa)** benefits from the Busch Gardens setting and a splashdown finale. It is shorter than Valravn but has a tighter, more focused layout.

**Griffon (Busch Gardens Williamsburg)** is basically SheiKra's twin with a slightly different layout and a stunning setting overlooking the Rhine River section of the park.

**The consensus:** Dive coasters are the most divisive B&M type. Many enthusiasts consider them one-trick ponies (the holding brake is amazing, the rest of the ride is average). Valravn has the best stats. SheiKra has the best overall experience. Nobody hates dives, but few people rank them as their favorite B&M type.

## Wing Coasters

Riders sit in seats extending from the sides of the track, with nothing above or below them. Wing coasters are about the sense of freedom and the near-miss elements (flying past structures, through keyholes, and over terrain).

**Community top picks:**

**The Swarm (Thorpe Park, UK)** gets high marks for theming (you fly through a crashed-airplane fuselage and past a destroyed church) and for a compact layout that uses its elements well.

**GateKeeper (Cedar Point)** is the most visible B&M wing in North America. It straddles the park entrance with a keyhole element. The ride is smooth and photogenic, but enthusiasts generally rate it as a mid-tier experience. The forces are mild.

**Thunderbird (Holiday World)** is the first launched B&M wing coaster. The launch adds intensity that chain-lift wings lack, and Holiday World's atmosphere makes everything better.

**Wild Eagle (Dollywood)** uses Dollywood's mountain setting effectively. The views are exceptional.

**The consensus:** Wing coasters are polarizing. Enthusiasts who value sensation and forces tend to rank them low. Enthusiasts who value the overall experience (views, theming, sense of flight) rank them higher. No wing coaster dominates community rankings the way Montu dominates inverts.

## Floorless Coasters

The floorless experience (sitting in an open car with no floor, similar to an invert but sitting above the track) is a B&M specialty that peaked in popularity in the early 2000s. The type has fallen out of fashion somewhat as parks prefer hypers and gigas.

**Community top picks:**

**Kraken (SeaWorld Orlando)** is the enthusiast favorite. It has a strong layout with multiple inversions, good pacing, and a tunnel section. The underwater-themed trains are distinctive.

**Bizarro/Medusa (Six Flags Great Adventure)** has been through multiple name changes but the ride hardware is still strong. The zero-G roll is a highlight.

**Dominator (Kings Dominion)** was relocated from Geauga Lake and is one of the stronger floorless installations. The vertical loop is massive.

**The consensus:** Floorless coasters are well-liked but rarely anyone's favorite B&M type. Kraken leads the category.

## Stand-Up Coasters

B&M stopped building stand-ups years ago, and most installations have been converted to floorless (Rougarou at Cedar Point, Apocalypse at Six Flags America). The few remaining stand-ups have a cult following among enthusiasts who enjoy the unique riding position despite the roughness.

**Riddler's Revenge (Six Flags Magic Mountain)** is the last significant B&M stand-up and is frequently called the best stand-up coaster ever built. The layout is aggressive, the inversions are smooth for the type, and the riding position creates a different sensation than any sit-down coaster.

**Georgia Scorcher (Six Flags Over Georgia)** is smaller but well-regarded for its speed and compactness.

**The consensus:** Riddler's Revenge is the best stand-up, and it is not close. Most enthusiasts would prefer the type keep existing rather than see the remaining installations converted.

## The Overall Verdict

If you forced the community to rank B&M's product lines from best to worst:

1. **Hyper/Giga** -- the flagship product, consistently delivers
2. **Inverted** -- the ride type they perfected
3. **Floorless** -- solid but less exciting than the top two
4. **Dive** -- the first drop is S-tier, everything else is B-tier
5. **Wing** -- great for the GP, mid for enthusiasts
6. **Stand-up** -- extinct but respected

B&M does not build the craziest rides in the world. They build the most reliable ones. And that consistency is why parks keep ordering them and enthusiasts keep riding them.
