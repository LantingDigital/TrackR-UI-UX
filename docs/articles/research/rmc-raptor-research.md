## Key Facts

### Family Kingdom Raptor Specs
- Location: Family Kingdom Amusement Park, Myrtle Beach, South Carolina (oceanfront, Ocean Blvd)
- Opening: Late Summer 2026 (closer to Labor Day than Memorial Day per Coaster101)
- Height: 100 ft (about 30 ft taller than Swamp Fox)
- Speed: 52 mph
- Track length: 1,920 ft
- Inversions: 3
- Airtime moments: 5
- Layout lineage: Very similar to Fire Runner at Lost Island but with extra airtime hill at end and final brake run flipped (left into station instead of right)
- Trains: Single-rail Raptor track with inline single-seat cars (1 rider per row)
- Name: Still unannounced. Park hint: "We're near the ocean, use your imagination."
- Track shipments: "first of 28 deliveries" arriving early March 2026
- Will be Southeast's first RMC single-rail Raptor
- Family Kingdom's 4th coaster
- Coincides with Family Kingdom's 60th anniversary

### IAAPA Announcement
- Announced at IAAPA Expo 2025 in Orlando
- Called "one of the most surprising reveals" because it paired RMC with a small seaside independent park
- Dean Corl of Family Kingdom spoke with Funworld about the partnership

### RMC Company History
- Founded 2001 by Fred Grubb
- First fame: Topper Track and I-Box (Iron Horse) hybrid conversions
- Started with New Texas Giant (Six Flags Over Texas, 2011)
- 2010s dominated by large-scale conversions for major chains
- Key projects: Iron Rattler, Twisted Colossus, Steel Vengeance, Iron Gwazi, Zadra
- Alan Schilke: key design partner, famous for Arrow work and 4D coasters, signature inversions
- Fred Grubb: founder/president, construction-centric side

### RMC's Strategic Pivot
- Raptor and T-Rex single-rail concepts introduced at IAAPA 2015
- Raptor created as more compact, lower-cost alternative to T-Rex
- Aimed at local/regional parks that couldn't afford giant T-Rex hypercoasters
- Evidence of pivot to smaller parks:
  1. Stunt Pilot at Silverwood (RMC's "home" park)
  2. ArieForce One at Fun Spot America Atlanta (small chain, go-kart/flat-ride operations)
  3. Fire Runner at Lost Island (very small standalone theme park)
  4. Family Kingdom Raptor (modest oceanfront park)
- Six Flags-Cedar Fair merger raised questions about large chain CapEx cycles
- Raptors allow: lower cost/footprint, decent throughput, high marketability, modular layout reuse
- YouTube analysis: Raptors could "save RMC" by creating repeatable installations

### Raptor Product Line Split (enthusiast view)
1. "Original compact layout" (RailBlazer, Golden Lasso)
2. "Extended" custom Raptors (Jersey Devil, WW Flight of Courage, Fire Runner, Family Kingdom)
3. Experimental variants (YoY 6-inversion configuration)
