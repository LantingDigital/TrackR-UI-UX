## Key Facts Extracted

### Overview
- $50+ million investment, largest single attraction investment in Dollywood history
- Mack Rides manufacturer, working with Dollywood and Herschend Creative Studios
- World's first indoor family hybrid coaster and whitewater river raft ride
- Four ride "modes" in a single experience
- Located in Wildwood Grove, new building themed as Hidden Hollow Aviation hangar

### Location & Building
- Expansion plot behind/beyond existing Wildwood Grove midway, accessed past the Wildwood Tree area
- Show building presents as a large "green aircraft hangar" from guest paths and the Dollywood Express
- Approximately 44,000 sq ft, fully enclosed and temperature-controlled
- Year-round operation independent of Smoky Mountain weather

### Ride System
- Duration: ~5.5 minutes from dispatch to unload
- Capacity: ~1,200 riders per hour (slightly higher than Big Bear Mountain)
- Height requirement: 39 inches (family-accessible)
- Custom amphibious ride vehicles that traverse coaster track AND water channels
- Themed as the Oakley family's experimental aircraft/raft/boat
- Onboard: synchronized audio, interactivity with off-board audio, integrated lighting, interfaces with large-format projection surfaces

### Four Experience Segments
1. Nighttime flight over the Smoky Mountains (coaster-style section with aerial visuals)
2. Whitewater rafting through 500,000+ gallons of surging water
3. Mountain ridge coaster segment (more traditional coaster track)
4. Boat navigation across a mysterious shimmering lake

### Theme & Story
- Hidden Hollow Aviation: fictional hangar operation in Wildwood Grove
- Run by twin scientists Cora and Jasper Oakley, niece and nephew of explorer Ned Oakley
- The Bioluminator: the twins' invention that riders use
- Goal: search for Secret Lake, believed to feed the glowing roots of the Wildwood Tree
- Bioluminescence motif: Smokies at night - fireflies, starlight, glowing roots and water
- Dolly's comments about the mountains "coming to life" after dark

### Queue
- Inside the hangar: lab spaces, aviation gear, narrative set-ups
- Explains Hidden Hollow Aviation, the Oakley family, and the Bioluminator
- Track and vehicles revealed in load area
- Expected ~55-minute wait, designed to be entertaining with story moments and scenic reveals

### Dark Ride Elements
- Large sections in darkness with projection-mapped environments
- Practical scenic rockwork and forest
- Special lighting simulating bioluminescent flora/fauna
- Synchronized audio to blend coaster and water movements with story beats
- Four "chapters" structured as single continuous storyline, not discrete ride systems

### Construction & Timeline
- Announced September 17, 2025 (also referenced as September 25 in some sources)
- Building enclosed with active interior work as of March 2026
- Working "seven days a week, two shifts a day"
- NOT ready for March 13, 2026 park opening day
- Officially "Spring 2026" opening
- Internal aim before mid-June 2026
- Enthusiast channels suggest late spring rather than early

### Awards
- Mack Rides ride system received 2025 Amusement Today Golden Ticket for Best New Innovation

### Dollywood Context
- Park's 41st year (2026 season)
- Record attendance in 2025
- Herschend Family Entertainment owns Dollywood
